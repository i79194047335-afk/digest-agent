## Risk and Market Dynamics: Arbitrage on DEXs

- Professional searcher competition drives arbitrage spreads toward zero, making profitability heavily dependent on latency and gas efficiency.
- Impermanent loss can erode profits when the underlying AMM pool's price shifts between the arbitrage entry and exit legs.
- Slippage on large trades reduces captured spread, requiring careful sizing to avoid eating through the arbitrage margin.
- Sandwich attackers can front-run arbitrage transactions, inflating the purchase price and stealing part of the profit.
- Regulatory uncertainty around MEV extraction leaves arbitrage bots operating in a legal gray zone across multiple jurisdictions.
