> Topic: Arbitrage on DEXs

## Arbitrage Mechanisms

DEX arbitrage takes several forms, each exploiting a different type of pricing inefficiency that creates opportunities. Atomic arbitrage bundles purchase and sale into a single transaction, guaranteeing full reversion if profit cannot be captured, eliminating execution risk. Cross-exchange arbitrage scans the same token pair across multiple DEXs, buying low on one venue and selling high on another within one atomic execution. Triangular arbitrage operates within a single exchange, cycling through three tokens to profit when the implied cross-rate between any two deviates from the direct quoted rate. Flash-loan-powered arbitrage removes the capital barrier entirely: the searcher borrows the entire trade size in one transaction, executes the arbitrage, repays the loan plus fee, and keeps the residual profit, all without holding collateral upfront. Multi-hop arbitrage extends the triangular pattern through additional intermediate tokens, capturing accumulated mispricing across a chain of pools whose individual price distortions compound into a larger aggregate opportunity.

## Technical Execution Infrastructure

Capturing DEX arbitrage profit requires more than identifying a price gap: it demands a high-performance technical stack capable of outrunning fierce and well-funded competition. Mempool monitoring bots continuously scan pending transactions, simulating their market impact to detect emergent arbitrage opportunities in real time before competitors can react. Priority gas auctions let searchers bid escalating transaction fees to secure first execution, making gas strategy as important as trade logic itself. Private relay networks such as Flashbots allow searchers to submit bundles directly to block builders, bypassing the public mempool entirely and eliminating the risk of being front-run by rival bots watching the same pending transaction queue. Proposer-builder separation formalises this dynamic: validators auction block construction rights to specialised builders who order searcher bundles to maximise extracted value. Smart contract optimisation reduces gas overhead on each arbitrage leg, improving net profit even when gross price spreads are thin and competition is fierce.

## Risk and Market Dynamics

The profitability of DEX arbitrage is under constant pressure from structural risks and intensifying competitive pressure among sophisticated and well-capitalized participants worldwide. Professional searcher firms deploy co-located infrastructure and dedicated capital pools, driving arbitrage spreads toward zero and making profitability heavily dependent on latency advantages measured in mere milliseconds. Impermanent loss can erode returns when the underlying pool price shifts between the arbitrage entry and exit legs, effectively offsetting the captured spread entirely. Slippage on large trades shrinks the realised margin, requiring precise position sizing to avoid eating through the opportunity completely. Sandwich attackers present a persistent adversarial risk: they detect arbitrage transactions in the mempool, front-run them to inflate the purchase price, and extract part of the profit that would otherwise go to the arbitrageur. Regulatory uncertainty around MEV extraction further complicates the landscape, leaving bots operating in a legal gray zone across multiple jurisdictions with evolving policy frameworks.
