## Context: decentralized finance

Decentralized finance emerged from the broader cryptocurrency movement that began with Bitcoin in 2009. While Bitcoin demonstrated that trustless value transfer was possible without banks, it lacked programmability. Ethereum, proposed by Vitalik Buterin in 2013 and launched in 2015, introduced smart contracts — self-executing code running on a public blockchain — which provided the infrastructure for DeFi.

Early experiments like MakerDAO (2017) and Uniswap (2018) proved that core financial primitives — lending, borrowing, and exchange — could be replicated in code without intermediaries. The "DeFi Summer" of 2020 catalyzed mass adoption through yield farming incentives, attracting both retail and institutional participants.

**Main players:** Aave and Compound dominate decentralized lending. Uniswap and Curve Finance lead decentralized exchange. MakerDAO/Sky manages the largest decentralized stablecoin. Layer-2 networks such as Arbitrum and Optimism reduce transaction costs, expanding accessibility.

**Problems it solves:** DeFi removes the need for trusted intermediaries (banks, brokers, clearinghouses), enabling permissionless access to financial services for anyone with an internet connection. It eliminates geographic barriers, reduces settlement times from days to seconds, and offers transparent, auditable rules encoded in smart contracts. For the 1.4 billion unbanked adults globally, it presents an alternative path to credit, savings, and exchange without requiring identity documents or credit history.
