## Practice: decentralized finance

### Example 1 — Aave: Permissionless Lending at Scale
**Who:** Aave Protocol (originally ETHLend, rebranded 2018), used by hundreds of thousands of individual and institutional borrowers globally.
**What:** Aave allows users to deposit crypto assets into liquidity pools and earn interest, or borrow against collateral without credit checks. In 2020–2021, Aave introduced "flash loans" — uncollateralized loans that must be borrowed and repaid within a single transaction block — enabling arbitrage and liquidation bots to operate without upfront capital.
**Result:** Aave grew to over $10 billion TVL at peak. Flash loans demonstrated a genuinely novel financial instrument with no equivalent in traditional finance, processing billions of dollars in single-transaction loans.

### Example 2 — MakerDAO / DAI: A Decentralized Stablecoin
**Who:** MakerDAO, a DAO governed by MKR token holders, serving users who need price-stable assets on-chain.
**What:** Users lock ETH or other accepted collateral into Maker Vaults at a minimum 150% collateralization ratio to mint DAI, a soft-pegged $1 stablecoin. DAI can then be used across DeFi protocols, sent globally, or held as a stable store of value. When collateral ratios fall below threshold, smart contracts automatically liquidate positions to protect the peg.
**Result:** DAI has maintained its dollar peg through multiple extreme market downturns including the March 2020 COVID crash and the 2022 crypto bear market, demonstrating the robustness of algorithmic, over-collateralized stablecoin design at scale.

### Example 3 — Uniswap v3: Concentrated Liquidity AMM
**Who:** Uniswap Labs and its decentralized liquidity providers, ranging from individual retail LPs to professional market-making firms.
**What:** Uniswap v3 (launched May 2021) introduced concentrated liquidity, allowing LPs to allocate capital within specific price ranges rather than uniformly across all prices. This dramatically improved capital efficiency — LPs could achieve up to 4000× greater capital efficiency compared to v2 on stable pairs.
**Result:** Uniswap became the dominant on-chain exchange venue, regularly surpassing centralized exchanges in daily volume for certain token pairs. By 2023, Uniswap had facilitated over $1.5 trillion in cumulative trading volume since launch, with no central operator, custody of funds, or KYC requirement.
