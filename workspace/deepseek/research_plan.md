## Research plan: Arbitrage on DEXs

### Reasoning
DEX arbitrage spans three distinct, non-overlapping dimensions: the mechanical underpinnings (how AMMs create exploitable price discrepancies), the execution layer (strategies, bots, and tooling used to capture those opportunities), and the economic reality (profitability constraints, risks, and systemic effects on liquidity providers and markets). Together these angles give a complete picture from theory to practice to consequences.

### Angles
1. AMM Price Mechanics — How automated market makers generate price discrepancies across pools and what conditions make them arbitrageable.
2. Execution Strategies & Tooling — How arbitrageurs detect and capture opportunities using bots, flash loans, gas bidding, and MEV infrastructure.
3. Economics & Risks — The profitability calculus of DEX arb: gas costs, failed transactions, MEV competition, and impact on liquidity providers.
