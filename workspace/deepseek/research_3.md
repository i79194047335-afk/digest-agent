## Economics & Risks: Arbitrage on DEXs
- Gas costs on Ethereum often exceed arb profits, making most opportunities economically unviable without batching.
- Failed transactions still consume gas, so bots pay full execution costs even on losing arbitrage attempts.
- MEV competition drives priority gas auctions, compressing net arb margins as bots overbid to win blocks.
- Liquidity providers silently subsidize arbitrageurs through adverse selection, suffering losses whenever prices stale against them.
- Sandwich attacks extract value from ordinary traders, indirectly raising the cost floor arbitrageurs must exceed to profit.
