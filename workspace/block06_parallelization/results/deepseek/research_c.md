## Practice: decentralized finance in action

**Example 1: Uniswap — Permissionless Token Exchange**
Uniswap, created by Hayden Adams in 2018, replaced the traditional exchange order book with automated market maker pools. Anyone can deposit token pairs into a pool and earn trading fees proportional to their share, or swap tokens directly against the pool at algorithmically determined prices. By early 2024, Uniswap had processed over $1.7 trillion in cumulative trading volume across multiple blockchains, making it one of the most used DeFi applications. The result: a global exchange with no listing requirements, no central operator, and no custody of user funds — accessible to anyone with a crypto wallet.

**Example 2: MakerDAO and DAI — Decentralized Lending and Stable Value**
MakerDAO allows users to lock volatile crypto assets like ETH as collateral and mint DAI, a stablecoin soft-pegged to the US dollar. As of mid-2024, over $5 billion in DAI was in circulation, backed by billions in collateral held in transparent smart contracts. This system provides borrowers with dollar-denominated liquidity without selling their assets, while DAI holders get a stable medium of exchange that operates entirely outside the traditional banking system. MakerDAO demonstrated that algorithmic stability mechanisms, combined with over-collateralization and governance, can maintain a peg without a central issuer.

**Example 3: Aave — Algorithmic Lending and Flash Loans**
Aave, originally launched as ETHLend in 2017 and rebranded in 2020, pioneered flash loans: uncollateralized loans that must be borrowed and repaid within a single blockchain transaction. If the loan is not repaid by the transaction's end, the entire operation reverses as if it never happened. This innovation enabled arbitrage, collateral swapping, and debt refinancing strategies impossible in traditional finance. In 2021, Aave processed over $3 billion in flash loan volume, and the protocol had grown to secure more than $10 billion in total deposits across multiple blockchain networks by its 2024 peak, showcasing how programmable money creates entirely new financial primitives.
